Spaghetti Carbonara

Luscious and indulgent, spaghetti carbonara combines crispy pancetta, eggs, cheese and black pepper for a silky sauce. Adapted from Simply Recipes【876462307663677†L115-L124】【876462307663677†L234-L249】.

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil or unsalted butter
  • ½ pound pancetta or thick‑cut bacon, diced
  • 1–2 garlic cloves, minced (optional)
  • 3–4 whole eggs
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 pound spaghetti (or bucatini or fettuccine)
  • Kosher salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

Method

  1. Put a large pot of salted water on to boil (about one tablespoon of salt per two quarts of water).
  2. Heat the olive oil or butter in a sauté pan over medium heat. Add the pancetta or bacon and cook slowly until crispy. Stir in the garlic, if using, and cook for another minute, then transfer the pancetta and garlic to a large bowl.
  3. In a small bowl, beat the eggs and stir in about half of the cheese.
  4. Add the pasta to the boiling water and cook until al dente.
  5. When the pasta is al dente, use tongs to transfer it to the bowl with the pancetta and garlic. Let the pasta be dripping wet and reserve some of the pasta water.
  6. Toss everything together, allowing the pasta to cool slightly so the eggs won’t scramble. Quickly mix in the beaten egg mixture to form a creamy sauce. Add reserved pasta water as needed to loosen the sauce and stir in the remaining cheese. Season with salt and plenty of black pepper and serve immediately.
